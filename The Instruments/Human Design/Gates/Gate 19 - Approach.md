# Gate 19: Approach (Wanting)

## Ra's Definition
According to Ra Uru Hu, Gate 19 is located in the Root Center and represents **the pressure to approach others with sensitivity to their needs**—the recognition that all things are interrelated, that resources must be shared to ensure tribal survival. This is not "wanting" in the sense of personal desire, but rather **the pressure to be needed and to meet needs**.

Ra taught that Gate 19 operates within the **Tribal Ego Circuit**, which means it carries **pressure for material support and resource exchange**. This gate enables the individual to recognize what others need and to approach them with offerings of support—but this approach is driven by the Root's pressure, creating a dynamic where the individual feels compelled to be needed in order to ensure their own place in the tribe.

**"Gate 19 is about sensitivity to needs—not your own needs, but the needs of others. You feel pressure to approach, to offer, to be the one who provides what's missing. But this is tribal—it's about survival. You want to be needed because being needed means you have a place, you have security. This is the gate of the caretaker who can't stop caring because stopping means being alone."** — Ra Uru Hu[^1]

The keynote here is **pressure to approach with sensitivity to needs**. This is the Root Center's pressure applied to **tribal interdependence**, the recognition that survival requires mutual support and that one's value is proven through meeting others' needs.

## I-Ching Hexagram Analysis

Hexagram 19, **臨 (Lín) - Approach / Nearing**, is composed of Earth (trigram Kun) above Lake (trigram Dui). This creates the image of **the earth approaching the lake**—gentle, receptive approach toward what is needed, the recognition that all things draw near to sustain one another.

**Judgment (Wilhelm/Baynes):**
> "Approach has supreme success. Perseverance furthers. When the eighth month comes, there will be misfortune."

The I-Ching reveals that **approach must be timely and sensitive**—that drawing near to others requires awareness of seasons and cycles. When Earth (receptivity, nourishment) rests above Lake (joy, responsiveness), the result is **gentle approach** that honors needs without force. But the warning of the eighth month suggests that **approach cannot be sustained indefinitely**—there are seasons for drawing near and seasons for withdrawal.

**Image:**
> "The earth above the lake: the image of Approach. Thus the superior man is inexhaustible in his will to teach, and without limits in his tolerance and protection of the people."

The trigram structure (Earth above Lake) suggests that **approach is an act of teaching and protection**—the capacity to *draw near* to those in need and offer resources without expectation of return. This is tribal caretaking, the recognition that the strong approach the weak to ensure collective survival.

**Nuclear Hexagram:** Hexagram 24 (Return)—revealing that beneath approach lies **the return to source**, the recognition that one approaches others to return to the tribal foundation, to reconnect with the interdependence that ensures survival.

In Human Design terms, this hexagram's placement in the **Root Center** transforms the I-Ching's gentle approach into **pressured sensitivity**. The question becomes: "Can I approach others with sensitivity to their needs without losing myself in co-dependence?"

## Line-by-Line Breakdown

### Line 1: Interdependence
**Exalted (Moon):** Natural recognition that all survival is interdependent, that approaching others with support is mutual exchange, not one-sided giving.
**Detriment (Uranus):** Resentment of interdependence, approaching others begrudgingly, creating obligation rather than genuine support.

This line reveals that **approach begins with acknowledging interdependence**—the recognition that tribal survival requires mutual support, that needing and being needed are not weaknesses but survival strategies. The individual with this line learns that approach is exchange, not sacrifice.

### Line 2: Service
**Exalted (Saturn):** Disciplined service to tribal needs, approaching others with reliability and consistency. Service as duty and honor.
**Detriment (Neptune):** Service that becomes servitude, approaching others from obligation or guilt rather than genuine sensitivity to needs.

The second line bridges interdependence (line 1) with action. When the individual approaches from genuine service, they strengthen the tribe. When detriment, service becomes exhausting self-sacrifice that breeds resentment.

### Line 3: Dedication
**Exalted (Earth):** Sustained dedication to meeting needs, approaching others repeatedly through cycles of giving and receiving. Grounded commitment.
**Detriment (Uranus):** Erratic dedication that creates instability, approaching when convenient and withdrawing when tired, undermining tribal trust.

Line 3 is the trial-and-error foundation. This individual *experiments with approach* to discover which forms of support are sustainable and which lead to depletion. Through repeated cycles, they learn the rhythm of giving and withdrawing.

### Line 4: The Team Player
**Exalted (Jupiter):** The capacity to approach the collective as a team member, offering support that strengthens group cohesion without dominating.
**Detriment (Pluto):** Using approach to manipulate group dynamics, offering support to gain power or control rather than to serve collective needs.

The fourth line transpersonal shift: at this line, the individual recognizes that **approach serves the team**, not just individuals. The shadow is using sensitivity to needs as a strategy for dominance.

### Line 5: Sacrifice
**Exalted (Sun):** The capacity to approach with genuine sacrifice—offering resources or support even when it costs personally, trusting that tribal exchange will balance over time.
**Detriment (Earth):** Martyrdom disguised as sacrifice, approaching with resentment, using giving as a weapon to create obligation and guilt in others.

Line 5 is the heretic/savior dynamic applied to approach. The individual with this line carries a projection that their sensitivity to needs will **save the tribe**—that their approach will be the solution to collective suffering. When exalted, they sacrifice wisely. When detriment, they become the martyr.

### Line 6: The Recluse
**Exalted (Venus):** The wisdom to withdraw from approach when necessary, recognizing that not all needs are meant to be met, that retreat is sometimes the most sensitive response.
**Detriment (Mars):** Withdrawal that becomes isolation, refusing to approach even when tribal survival requires it, abandoning interdependence out of fear or exhaustion.

The sixth line culminates in **wisdom through withdrawal**. After the trials of lines 1-5, the individual recognizes that **true sensitivity includes knowing when not to approach**—that sometimes the most loving response is to allow others to meet their own needs.

## Circuitry Analysis

Gate 19 belongs to the **Tribal Ego Circuit**, one of two circuits within the broader Tribal stream (Ego/Support and Defense). The Ego Circuit is characterized by:

- **Material Support:** Providing and receiving resources to ensure tribal survival
- **Bargains and Agreements:** Exchange-based relationships rooted in mutual need
- **Sensitivity to Needs:** Awareness of what others require for survival and wellbeing
- **Pressure for Security:** The drive to establish and maintain tribal bonds through support

**The Role of Gate 19 in the Circuit:**

Gate 19 is the **initiating pressure** of the Tribal Ego Circuit—the Root Center's pressure to *approach others with sensitivity* to their needs. However, without connection to Gate 49 (Revolution, Solar Plexus), this pressure lacks **emotional clarity about relationships**—the individual feels compelled to approach but may not know *which* relationships are correct or sustainable.

**Why Tribal Survival Requires Approach:**

Because the Tribal Ego Circuit is designed to **ensure material support and resource exchange**, Gate 19's approach is essential—it initiates the contact that allows needs to be met. The individual approaches not from altruism but from **tribal pressure**—the recognition that being needed ensures one's place in the collective.

## Channel Formation

Gate 19 forms **Channel 19-49: Synthesis (The Channel of Sensitivity)** when connected to Gate 49 in the Solar Plexus Center.

#### Electromagnetic Function

- **Gate 19 (Root):** "I feel pressure to approach others with sensitivity to their needs and to be needed in return."
- **Gate 49 (Solar Plexus):** "I have emotional clarity about which relationships are correct, which needs are mine to meet."
- **Channel 19-49:** The bridge between pressure to approach and emotional discernment—the capacity to **form correct tribal bonds** based on mutual need.

When this channel is defined, the individual has **consistent access to sensitive approach**. They are designed to recognize needs and form relationships based on resource exchange and mutual support. This is the channel of **the caretaker, the tribal supporter, the one who ensures everyone's needs are met**—but also the one who must navigate the emotional complexity of interdependence.

**Without the channel defined** (only Gate 19 activated), the individual experiences pressure to approach but may lack emotional clarity about *which* needs to meet. They need another person with Gate 49 to complete the electromagnetic circuit—someone who can provide emotional discernment about relationships.

#### Interpersonal Electromagnetic Dynamics

- **Both have Channel 19-49:** Potential mutual caretaking or conflict over whose needs are being met. Can be profoundly nurturing if both honor balance.
- **One has Gate 19, other has Gate 49:** The Gate 19 person provides pressured approach; the Gate 49 person provides emotional clarity. This can create balanced partnership—action and discernment.
- **Neither has this channel:** The pressure to approach remains latent potential, accessed only when in proximity to someone with Gate 49.

## Biological and Somatic Dimension

Gate 19 is located in the **Root Center**, which corresponds biologically to the **adrenal glands**—the stress response system that produces adrenaline and cortisol in response to pressure.

#### Physiological Signature

Individuals with Gate 19 defined may experience:
- A **somatic pressure to approach**—the body feels restless, driven to reach out, to offer support, to be needed
- **Adrenal activation** when needs are unmet (either their own need to be needed or others' needs)—stress hormones create urgency
- A felt sense of **hypervigilance to needs**—constantly scanning the environment for who requires support
- **Exhaustion** when approach is not balanced with withdrawal—adrenal depletion from constant giving

#### Energetic Signature

The Root Center radiates **pressure and stress** into the aura. When Gate 19 is active, the aura carries a frequency that says: "I am here to sense your needs and approach with support. I need to be needed." Others may feel this as **nurturing**, **attentive**, or (if the individual is in Not-Self) **intrusive** or **co-dependent**.

**Defined vs. Undefined in Gate 19:**

- **Defined Gate 19:** Consistent pressure to approach and be needed. The individual knows they are here to support others, though they may need Gate 49 to know *which* relationships are correct.
- **Undefined/Open Gate 19:** Samples pressure to approach from others. Vulnerable to conditioning around "needing to be needed" or "proving worth through caretaking." Can become wise about interdependence by observing it in others.

## Strategy Integration
Gate 19, being in the Root Center, does not carry Authority (only Sacral, Solar Plexus, Splenic, Ego, Self-Projected, and No-Inner-Authority are Authority centers). However, **it creates pressure that must be filtered through Authority**.

**For Generators/Manifesting Generators with Gate 19:**

The pressure to approach must still wait for the Sacral response. The Gate 19 energy says "They need help!" but the Sacral must respond to confirm whether *this particular approach* is correct. Not every need is theirs to meet.

**For Projectors with Gate 19:**

The sensitivity to needs is clear, but the Projector must wait for invitation before approaching with support. Uninvited caretaking can lead to bitterness. The gift is knowing *when* to offer sensitivity and *when* to wait.

**For Manifestors with Gate 19:**

The pressure to approach can be initiated, but the Manifestor must **inform** before offering support that may impact others. Informing allows others to receive the approach consciously.

**For Reflectors with Gate 19:**

The pressure to approach is inconsistent (activated only by transits or others). The Reflector must wait the full lunar cycle before committing to caretaking relationships, ensuring the approach aligns with their deep timing.

## Not-Self Patterns
**Shadow Expressions of Gate 19:**

- **Co-dependence:** Needing to be needed so intensely that one's identity becomes fused with caretaking, losing self in service to others' needs.
- **Martyrdom:** Approaching with resentment, using sensitivity to needs as a weapon to create guilt and obligation.
- **Intrusive Support:** Approaching before being invited, forcing help on others who have not asked for it, creating discomfort.
- **Burnout:** Attempting to meet all needs without withdrawal, depleting adrenal resources through constant approach.
- **Manipulation Through Needs:** Using sensitivity to needs to control others, creating dependence to ensure one's own security.
- **Resentful Giving:** Approaching out of pressure or obligation rather than genuine sensitivity, breeding bitterness.

#### Not-Self Indicators

- Frustration from approaching without Sacral response (for Generators/MGs)
- Bitterness from offering support without invitation (for Projectors)
- Exhaustion from constant caretaking without balance
- Feeling unappreciated despite constant giving

**Conditioning Patterns for Undefined Gate 19:**

Those without Gate 19 may be conditioned to believe:
- "I must be needed to have value."
- "I need to take care of everyone to be loved."
- "If I don't approach, I'm selfish or uncaring."

This conditioning leads to forcing caretaking in areas that are not mechanically theirs to support, or feeling guilty for honoring boundaries.

## Deconditioning Process

The **7-year deconditioning cycle** for Gate 19 involves learning to recognize when approach is authentic (aligned with sensitivity and correct relationships) versus conditioned (driven by pressure to prove worth or avoid abandonment).

**Year 1-2:** Awareness phase—noticing when approach feels genuine versus when it feels like obligation, performance, or fear-based caretaking.

**Year 3-4:** Experimentation phase—testing which relationships invite support and which drain energy, observing which forms of approach are sustainable.

**Year 5-6:** Refinement phase—beginning to trust emotional clarity (especially if Gate 49 is defined or accessed through relationship), honoring when to approach and when to withdraw.

**Year 7:** Integration phase—approach becomes aligned with Strategy and Authority. The individual approaches from **sensitivity** (Gene Keys Gift) rather than co-dependence. Support is offered when correct, and withdrawal is honored without guilt.

#### Somatic Practice

- Notice where the impulse to approach arises in the body. Is it the root (adrenal pressure)? The solar plexus (emotional need)? The gut (Sacral response)?
- Practice **discerning whose needs are yours to meet**—not all needs require your approach. Allow others their own process.
- Honor **rest and withdrawal**—the adrenals require recovery time. Constant approach creates depletion.
