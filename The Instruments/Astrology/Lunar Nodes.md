# The Lunar Nodes

*The Ascending and Descending Knot*

## Archetypal Essence

The lunar nodes are the two points where the Moon's orbital path intersects the ecliptic — the Sun's apparent path through the zodiac. They are not celestial bodies but mathematical points: invisible intersections that become visible only when they cause eclipses. The nodes are where Sun and Moon can meet in total alignment, where the luminaries' paths cross and, for a brief window, one can obscure the other[^1].

The Hellenistic astrologers called the nodes the *sundesmos* — the "bond" or "fastening" — the knot that ties the lunar orbit to the solar plane. They are the eclipse axis: the places where light can be temporarily extinguished. This astronomical reality determined their astrological meaning. The nodes do not signify psychological growth, soul purpose, or karmic lessons. They signify **increase and decrease** — the augmentation or diminishment of whatever they touch[^2].

The **Ascending Node** (*anabibazōn* — "mounting," "going up") is the point where the Moon crosses the ecliptic moving northward. It increases, amplifies, and augments. The **Descending Node** (*katabibazōn* — "dismounting," "coming down") is the point where the Moon crosses moving southward. It decreases, diminishes, and subtracts. One turns the volume up; the other turns it down.

## Astronomy of the Nodes

The Moon's orbit is tilted approximately 5° relative to the ecliptic. This tilt creates two intersection points:

| Node | Greek Term | Motion | Astronomical Meaning |
|------|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| **Ascending (North)** | *anabibazōn* | Moon crosses ecliptic moving north | Moon enters northern celestial latitude |
| **Descending (South)** | *katabibazōn* | Moon crosses ecliptic moving south | Moon enters southern celestial latitude |

The nodes are not fixed — they **retrograde** through the zodiac, completing a full cycle in approximately **18.6 years** (the Metonic-adjacent nodal cycle). This means the eclipse axis slowly rotates backward through all twelve signs, spending roughly 18 months in each sign pair.

**Eclipse mechanics:** An eclipse can only occur when a New Moon (solar eclipse) or Full Moon (lunar eclipse) happens near one of the nodes — when the Sun, Moon, and nodal axis are sufficiently aligned for one luminary to obscure the other. Eclipses occur in seasons, roughly every six months, as the Sun transits near the nodes[^3].

The later medieval tradition called the Ascending Node *Caput Draconis* (Head of the Dragon) and the Descending Node *Cauda Draconis* (Tail of the Dragon) — imagining a celestial serpent whose head devours the light at one node and releases it at the other. This imagery appears in Abu Ma'shar (9th century) and becomes standard in Renaissance astrology.

## Significations: Increase and Decrease

The core traditional signification is quantitative, not qualitative — the nodes modify the *magnitude* of what they contact, not its *nature*[^4].

### The Ascending Node (North Node)

**Increase, addition, augmentation.**

Whatever planet or point the Ascending Node conjoins is *amplified*. Jupiter with the North Node is more Jupiterian — more generous, more expansive, more excessive. Saturn with the North Node is more Saturnian — more structured, more restrictive, more burdensome. The node does not distinguish between pleasant and unpleasant qualities; it increases whatever is there.

Dorotheus (via Hephaestio) applies this in inception charts: when the Moon is with the Ascending Node, "the buyer in the market will pay a higher price" — quantity increases.

### The Descending Node (South Node)

**Decrease, subtraction, diminishment.**

Whatever planet or point the Descending Node conjoins is *reduced*. Its capacity to manifest is suppressed, muted, or drained. In the same Dorothean passage, the Descending Node indicates "decreasing in numbers" — the buyer pays less, the quantity shrinks.

### Rhetorius's Assignment

The later Hellenistic astrologer Rhetorius the Egyptian systematized the nodes' relationship to benefics and malefics[^5]:

| Configuration | Effect |
|--------------|--------|
| North Node + Benefic | The benefic is *more benefic* — its positive capacity amplified |
| North Node + Malefic | The malefic is *more malefic* — its harmful capacity amplified |
| South Node + Benefic | The benefic is *diminished* — its positive capacity suppressed |
| South Node + Malefic | The malefic is *diminished* — its harmful capacity reduced |

This creates a counterintuitive insight: **the South Node can be favorable with malefics** because it decreases their malice. Saturn or Mars with the South Node is Saturn or Mars with the volume turned down — less destructive, less overwhelming. Conversely, the North Node with a malefic is dangerous precisely because it amplifies what is already difficult.

This principle is echoed in the later tradition — Lilly assigns the North Node as benefic (increases fortune) and the South Node as malefic (decreases fortune), but the Hellenistic nuance of *decreasing maleficence* adds an important corrective.

## The Nodes and Eclipses

Valens treats the nodes with particular caution because of their role as the eclipse axis[^6].

### Valens's Warnings

Valens calls the nodes *dunastikos* — "powerful" — but warns strongly against beginning ventures (inception/electional charts) when the Moon is conjunct, square, or opposing the nodes. He associates the Moon passing through the "eclipsing places" (*ekleiptikon topon*) with outcomes that are:

- **Incomplete** — the venture does not reach fulfillment
- **Inconstant** — what is achieved does not last
- **Subject to punishment** — reversal or consequence follows

The underlying logic: the nodes are where light can be extinguished. Initiating action at these points means building on unstable ground — the foundation can be eclipsed.

### Eclipses in Natal Charts

A natal planet closely conjunct a node (especially within 3-5°) carries the eclipse signature: its significations are subject to periodic obscuration. The planet's capacity waxes and wanes more dramatically than other planets', punctuated by eclipse activations every ~9 years (half the nodal cycle) when the eclipse axis returns to the natal node's position.

Eclipses *transiting* over natal planets or points activate nodal themes — temporary obscuration followed by revelation, loss followed by unexpected return, or the dramatic amplification/diminishment of the eclipsed planet's topics.

## Natal Delineation

### By Sign and Ruler

Valens delineates the nodes through the **ruler of the sign** the Ascending Node occupies. The node itself is a mathematical point without inherent nature — its character depends entirely on the planet that rules its sign and that planet's natal condition[^7].

*Example:* North Node in Sagittarius → Jupiter rules the node. If Jupiter is angular, in sect, and bonified, the node's augmenting power operates constructively. If Jupiter is cadent, out of sect, and maltreated, the node amplifies but in a context of excess, poor judgment, or misplaced faith.

### Configuration with Planets

- **Planets conjunct the Ascending Node:** Amplified for better or worse (see Rhetorius's table above)
- **Planets conjunct the Descending Node:** Diminished — reduced capacity for outward manifestation
- **Benefics configured to nodes:** Rhetorius considers this an indicator of prosperity and effectiveness
- **No planets configured to nodes:** The nodes operate through their sign rulers alone

### The Nodal Axis in Houses

The nodes always occupy opposite signs (and therefore opposite houses in Whole Sign). The house axis they occupy identifies which life domains are subject to the increase/decrease dynamic:

| Axis | Increase (North Node) | Decrease (South Node) |
|------|----------------------|----------------------|
| 1st / 7th | Self amplified; partnerships diminished (or vice versa) |
| 2nd / 8th | Resources amplified; shared resources/death themes diminished |
| 4th / 10th | Home/family amplified; career/reputation diminished |
| 5th / 11th | Creativity amplified; community/friends diminished |
| 3rd / 9th | Communication amplified; philosophy/travel diminished |
| 6th / 12th | Daily labor amplified; isolation/hidden enemies diminished |

## What the Nodes Are Not

The traditional treatment of the lunar nodes diverges sharply from the evolutionary astrology framework popularized in the late 20th century. To maintain clarity:

- **The nodes are not a soul-purpose axis.** They do not indicate where the soul has been (South Node) or where it is going (North Node). This framework belongs to Jeffrey Wolf Green's *Evolutionary Astrology* (1994) and Steven Forrest's work — neither of which is grounded in the Hellenistic tradition.
- **The South Node is not "past-life baggage."** It is a point of decrease — diminishment of quantity and power. There is no karmic narrative attached to it in the original sources.
- **The North Node is not "the growth edge."** It is a point of increase — amplification. Growth, in the Jungian sense, happens across the entire chart through the process of [[Individuation Process|individuation]], not through a single axis.
- **The nodes do not replace the luminaries.** The Sun-Moon axis (conscious-unconscious, ego-Self) is the primary polarity of the chart. The nodes modify this axis through eclipses; they do not supersede it.

## Psychological Depth

The nodes, read through a Jungian lens, describe the psyche's relationship to **inflation and deflation** — the two dangers that bracket the individuation process.

**The Ascending Node** is the archetypal amplifier: it magnifies whatever complex, archetype, or psychic content it touches. This can be constructive (amplifying the ego's capacity for conscious engagement with an archetype) or destructive (inflating the ego's identification with the archetype until it loses its human proportions). Jupiter-North Node can mean expansive wisdom *or* grandiose inflation. Mars-North Node can mean amplified courage *or* unbounded aggression.

**The Descending Node** is the archetypal diminisher: it reduces the psychic content's capacity for conscious manifestation. This can be deflation (the ego loses access to resources it needs) or relief (an overwhelming complex finally loses its grip). Saturn-South Node can mean liberation from excessive rigidity *or* the loss of necessary structure.

**Eclipses** are the nodal archetype at its most dramatic — temporary but total obscuration of consciousness (solar eclipse = ego momentarily lost) or the unconscious (lunar eclipse = emotional content suddenly exposed). The eclipse does not destroy; it *interrupts* — and in the interruption, something shifts. What returns after the eclipse is never quite what was there before.

The traditional insight — increase and decrease, not purpose and destiny — keeps the nodes empirical. They describe a *dynamic*, not a narrative.

## Shadow Expression

- **Evolutionary inflation:** Reading the North Node as cosmic destiny and organizing the entire chart around it. This imports a framework alien to the tradition and distorts the chart's proportions — the Ascendant, luminaries, and chart ruler are far more central to identity than the nodes.
- **South Node shame:** Treating the South Node as something to "move away from" or overcome. In the traditional system, the South Node decreases — it does not condemn. Its conjunction with a malefic is actually *helpful* (reduced malice).
- **Eclipse panic:** Treating every eclipse as a crisis. Eclipses activate nodal themes, but their impact depends on whether they aspect natal planets and what condition those planets are in. Most eclipses are unremarkable for most people.
- **Ignoring the sign ruler:** Reading the nodes as self-sufficient points without examining the condition of the planet ruling the node's sign. The node is a mathematical point — its character comes entirely from its ruler.

## Interpretation Guide

### Assessing the Nodes in a Chart

1. **Locate the nodes** by sign and house (always opposite each other)
2. **Identify the rulers** of each node's sign
3. **Assess the rulers' condition** — the nodes operate through their rulers, not independently
4. **Check for planetary conjunctions** — apply Rhetorius's increase/decrease principle
5. **Note the house axis** — which life domains are subject to the amplification/diminishment dynamic?
6. **Check eclipse sensitivity** — are any natal planets within 3-5° of a node? They carry the eclipse signature

The nodes belong within the broader planetary assessment, typically examined alongside the luminaries:
