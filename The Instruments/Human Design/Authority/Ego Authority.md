# Ego Authority
*The Intelligence of Willpower*

---

## Ra's Definition
**Ego Authority** is defined by a **defined [[Heart]] Center** (also called the Ego or Will Center) *without* a defined [[Solar Plexus]], [[Sacral]], or [[Spleen]].[^1] If any of those centers is defined, they override the Heart in the authority hierarchy.

Ego Authority is the domain of a small subset of **[[Manifestor]]s** and **[[Projector]]s**—those who make decisions through willpower, commitment, and self-interest.

---

## The Two Subtypes

Ego Authority expresses differently depending on the Heart's connection:

### Ego-Manifested Authority
- **Configuration:** [[Heart]] connected directly to the [[Throat]][^2]
- **Type:** [[Manifestor]] only
- **Mechanics:** Truth is discovered by **speaking it aloud in the moment**
- **Somatic Experience:** You "blurt out" what you want; the act of speaking reveals your will

### Ego-Projected Authority
- **Configuration:** [[Heart]] connected to the [[G Center]] (Identity)[^3]
- **Type:** [[Projector]] only
- **Mechanics:** Truth is discovered by asking **"What do I want for myself?"** while waiting for an invitation
- **Somatic Experience:** You articulate your desires through self-inquiry and dialogue

---

## The Mechanics: Willpower and Commitment

Ego Authority operates through the **will to commit**. The Heart Center governs:[^4]
- **Material resources** (money, assets, possessions)
- **Promises and agreements** (the capacity to commit and follow through)
- **Self-worth and value** (knowing what you are worth)

Decisions made through Ego Authority are rooted in:
- **What I want** (self-interest, not selfishness)
- **What I am willing to commit to** (not what I "should" do)
- **What serves my material and egoic well-being** (not what serves others' expectations)

---

## Decision-Making Process

### For Ego-Manifested (Manifestors)

1. **Speak your truth aloud.** The Heart reveals what you want through the act of speaking.
2. **Notice what you "blurt out."** The first declaration (before the mind edits) is the true will.
3. **Inform after knowing.** Once you've spoken your will, inform those who will be impacted (Manifestor Strategy).

### For Ego-Projected (Projectors)

1. **Wait for the invitation.** (Projector Strategy)
2. **Ask yourself: "What do I want for myself?"** Speak this question aloud or in dialogue.
3. **Listen to your own articulation.** Your truth emerges through the sound of your voice.
4. **Commit only to what serves your will.** Do not accept invitations that require you to abandon your self-interest.

---

## Somatic Experience

Individuals with Ego Authority experience:
- **Willpower as clarity** (a sense of "I want this" or "I do not want this")
- **Commitment as truth** (if you are willing to commit, it is correct; if not, it is not)
- **Material grounding** (decisions tied to tangible resources and self-worth)
- **Self-interest as compass** (not selfish but self-honoring)

The Heart speaks through **declaration** (Ego-Manifested) or **self-inquiry** (Ego-Projected), not through emotional waves or gut responses.

---

## Interaction with Strategy

Ego Authority must be paired with [[Strategy]]:

- **[[Manifestor]] with Ego-Manifested:** Speak your will to discover it, then inform before acting
- **[[Projector]] with Ego-Projected:** Wait for the invitation, then ask "What do I want?" before accepting

Strategy positions you correctly; Ego Authority reveals *what* is correct for your will and material well-being.

---

## The Role of Rest

The Heart Center is a **motor**, but it is not sustainable like the [[Sacral]].[^5] Ego Authority requires:
- **Deep rest** between commitments
- **Honoring limits** (the Heart can exhaust if overused)
- **Selective engagement** (committing only to what is truly desired, not to everything)

Burnout occurs when Ego Authority individuals commit beyond their will's capacity to sustain.

---

## Compatible Types

Ego Authority can appear in:
- **Manifestors** (Ego-Manifested: Heart to Throat)
- **Projectors** (Ego-Projected: Heart to G Center)

This is a **rare configuration**, as most Manifestors and Projectors have Emotional or Splenic Authority.

---

## Common Pitfalls

### 1. Confusing Will with "Should"

Ego Authority is **what I want**, not **what I should do**. Conditioning often trains people to ignore their will in favor of duty, morality, or others' expectations.

### 2. Over-Committing

The Heart motor is powerful but not endless. Saying "yes" to everything depletes the will. Ego Authority requires **selective commitment**.

### 3. Ignoring Self-Interest

Ego Authority is designed to prioritize **self-interest** (what serves your material and egoic well-being). This is not selfishness—it is correct use of the will.

### 4. (For Ego-Projected) Accepting Invitations That Don't Serve You

Projectors with Ego Authority must wait for invitations, but not all invitations are correct. The question "What do I want for myself?" filters invitations through the will.

---

## The Gift of Ego Authority

Individuals with Ego Authority bring:
- **Commitment Power** — When they say "yes," they follow through
- **Material Mastery** — They understand value, resources, and worth
- **Self-Honoring** — They do not abandon their will to please others
- **Willful Clarity** — They know what they want and are not apologetic about it

## Strategy Integration

### For Those with Ego Authority

1. **(Ego-Manifested) Speak to know.** Blurt out what you want; your truth is revealed through declaration.
2. **(Ego-Projected) Ask yourself: "What do I want for myself?"** Let your voice reveal your will.
3. **Honor your self-interest.** You are designed to prioritize what serves you—this is not selfishness.
4. **Commit selectively.** Do not say "yes" to everything; your will has limits.
5. **Rest deeply.** The Heart motor requires recuperation between commitments.
6. **Do not override your will.** If you don't want it, do not force yourself to want it.

### For Those Around Ego Authority Individuals

1. **Respect their will.** When they say "I want this," do not argue or shame them.
2. **Do not pressure them to commit.** They know what they are willing to commit to; trust their discernment.
3. **Understand their self-interest.** They are designed to prioritize themselves—this is healthy, not selfish.
4. **Give them space to rest.** The Heart motor requires deep recuperation.
5. **(For Ego-Projected) Invite them formally.** They still require proper invitations (Projector Strategy).

---

*Ego Authority is the intelligence of the will—the recognition that your desires, commitments, and self-interest are not weaknesses but your compass. To know what you want and to honor it without apology is the practice of Ego Authority. The world will ask you to sacrifice your will for duty, for others, for "the right thing," but your Authority demands that you ask first: "What do I want for myself?"*
