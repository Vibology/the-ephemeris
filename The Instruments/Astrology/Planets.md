# The Planets
*The Archetypal Forces of Consciousness*

---

## Overview

The **planets** in astrology represent fundamental archetypal energies, drives, and principles of consciousness. Where the zodiac signs describe *how* energy expresses (qualities, elements, modalities), and the houses describe *where* energies manifest (life domains), the planets describe **what** energies are active—the actors on the stage of life.

Each planet embodies a specific function of the psyche or soul, from basic survival drives (Mars) to transcendent spiritual longings (Neptune). Planetary placements by sign, house, and aspect create the unique psychological fingerprint of the natal chart.

---

## The Two Luminaries

The **Sun** and **Moon** are technically not planets (the Sun is a star, the Moon Earth's satellite), but they are called the **luminaries** and hold special status in astrology:

### [[Sun ☉]] — The Conscious Self
**Archetype:** The Hero, The King, The Father
**Function:** Conscious identity, life purpose, creative vitality, individuation
**Traditional:** Hot, dry, masculine, diurnal
**Rulership:** Leo ♌

The Sun represents **who you are becoming**—the conscious self, the hero's journey, the drive toward individuation. It is the central organizing principle of the chart, the core identity around which all else revolves.

### [[Moon ☽]] — The Unconscious Self
**Archetype:** The Mother, The Child, The Nurturer
**Function:** Emotional nature, instincts, memory, needs, receptivity
**Traditional:** Cold, moist, feminine, nocturnal
**Rulership:** Cancer ♋

The Moon represents **who you have been**—the past, childhood, emotional patterns, instinctual reactions. It is the soul's memory, the container of feeling, the rhythm of inner tides.

## The Five Classical Planets

The **classical planets** (visible to the naked eye) were the foundation of ancient astrology for millennia:

### [[Mercury ☿]] — The Messenger
**Archetype:** The Trickster, The Scribe, The Merchant
**Function:** Communication, intellect, reason, perception, exchange
**Traditional:** Cold, dry, neutral (takes nature of planet it aspects)
**Rulership:** Gemini ♊, Virgo ♍

Mercury is the **mediator**—between conscious and unconscious, inner and outer, heaven and earth. It governs language, thought, learning, and the transmission of information.

### [[Venus ♀]] — The Lover
**Archetype:** The Lover, The Artist, The Peacemaker
**Function:** Love, beauty, harmony, values, desire, relationship
**Traditional:** Cold, moist, feminine, nocturnal (benefic)
**Rulership:** Taurus ♉, Libra ♎

Venus represents **attraction and connection**—what we value, what brings pleasure, how we relate. She is the principle of Eros, the unifier, the artist who sees beauty.

### [[Mars ♂]] — The Warrior
**Archetype:** The Warrior, The Soldier, The Champion
**Function:** Action, desire, conflict, courage, aggression, assertion
**Traditional:** Hot, dry, masculine, nocturnal (malefic)
**Rulership:** Aries ♈, Scorpio ♏ (traditional)

Mars is the **principle of severance**—cutting through, conquering, asserting will. He governs action, desire, anger, and the capacity to fight for what one wants.

### [[Jupiter ♃]] — The King
**Archetype:** The King, The Priest, The Sage
**Function:** Expansion, growth, wisdom, abundance, faith, meaning
**Traditional:** Hot, moist, masculine, diurnal (benefic)
**Rulership:** Sagittarius ♐, Pisces ♓ (traditional)

Jupiter represents **expansion and meaning**—growth, optimism, faith, the search for truth. He is the great benefic, bestowing abundance, wisdom, and good fortune.

### [[Saturn ♄]] — The Senex
**Archetype:** The Old Man, The Judge, The Taskmaster
**Function:** Limitation, structure, time, discipline, maturity, consequence
**Traditional:** Cold, dry, masculine, diurnal (malefic)
**Rulership:** Capricorn ♑, Aquarius ♒ (traditional)

Saturn is the **principle of form and limitation**—boundaries, time, responsibility, the hard lessons of reality. He is the great malefic, yet also the teacher of maturity and mastery.

## The Modern Planets

The **outer planets** (discovered through telescopes) represent transpersonal forces—collective, generational, evolutionary:

### [[Uranus ♅]] — The Awakener
**Archetype:** The Revolutionary, The Genius, The Liberator
**Function:** Awakening, revolution, innovation, sudden change, liberation
**Discovered:** 1781 (during American and French Revolutions)
**Rulership:** Aquarius ♒ (modern)

Uranus represents **sudden awakening and disruption**—lightning-bolt insights, revolutionary change, liberation from constraint. He breaks what Saturn built when it becomes rigid.

### [[Neptune ♆]] — The Dreamer
**Archetype:** The Mystic, The Artist, The Addict
**Function:** Dissolution, spirituality, imagination, illusion, transcendence
**Discovered:** 1846 (era of Romanticism, spiritualism, anesthesia)
**Rulership:** Pisces ♓ (modern)

Neptune represents **the dissolution of boundaries**—mystical union, artistic inspiration, but also confusion, delusion, escapism. He is the oceanic feeling, the longing to merge.

### [[Pluto ♇]] — The Underworld
**Archetype:** The Lord of the Underworld, The Phoenix, Death
**Function:** Transformation, death-rebirth, power, the unconscious, compulsion
**Discovered:** 1930 (era of depth psychology, fascism, atomic power)
**Rulership:** Scorpio ♏ (modern)

Pluto represents **death and regeneration**—the compulsion to destroy and rebuild, the descent into the underworld, the encounter with shadow and power. He is transformation through crisis.

## The Traditional Sect System

Traditional astrology divides planets into **diurnal** (day) and **nocturnal** (night) sects—planetary teams that work best in daytime or nighttime charts:

| Sect | Planets | Quality |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Diurnal (Day)** | Sun ☉, Jupiter ♃, Saturn ♄ | Hot, masculine, yang, active |
| **Nocturnal (Night)** | Moon ☽, Venus ♀, Mars ♂ | Cold, feminine, yin, receptive |
| **Neutral** | Mercury ☿ | Takes nature of planet it aspects |

**Sect dignity:** Planets of the day sect are strengthened in day charts (Sun above horizon at birth); planets of the night sect are strengthened in night charts (Sun below horizon). A planet in its proper sect is more beneficent; out of sect, more difficult.

## The Benefics and Malefics

Traditional astrology recognizes **inherent planetary nature**—some planets are naturally helpful (benefics), others naturally challenging (malefics):

### Benefics (Fortune-Bringers)
- **Jupiter ♃** — The Greater Benefic (expansion, abundance, wisdom)
- **Venus ♀** — The Lesser Benefic (pleasure, harmony, beauty)

### Malefics (Challenge-Bringers)
- **Saturn ♄** — The Greater Malefic (limitation, suffering, time)
- **Mars ♂** — The Lesser Malefic (conflict, aggression, severance)

### Neutral
- **Sun ☉, Moon ☽, Mercury ☿** — Neither inherently benefic nor malefic; function depends on condition

**Modern integration:** Psychological astrology emphasizes that malefics are not "bad"—they bring necessary challenges, discipline, and strength. Saturn teaches mastery; Mars teaches courage.

## Planetary Speed and Retrograde Motion

Planets move at different speeds and occasionally appear to move **retrograde** (backward) from Earth's perspective:

**Average speed (approximate):**
- **Moon ☽** — 13° per day (completes zodiac in 28 days)
- **Sun ☉** — 1° per day (completes zodiac in 365 days)
- **Mercury ☿** — 1-2° per day (retrograde 3x/year for ~3 weeks)
- **Venus ♀** — 1° per day (retrograde every 18 months for ~6 weeks)
- **Mars ♂** — 0.5° per day (retrograde every 2 years for ~2.5 months)
- **Jupiter ♃** — 0.1° per day (retrograde annually for ~4 months)
- **Saturn ♄** — 0.03° per day (retrograde annually for ~4.5 months)
- **Uranus ♅, Neptune ♆, Pluto ♇** — Extremely slow (retrograde nearly half the year)

**Retrograde meaning:** When retrograde, planetary energy turns **inward**—reflection, revision, reworking. Mercury retrograde disrupts communication; Venus retrograde reviews relationships; Mars retrograde turns aggression inward.

## Notes

Traditional astrology (Ptolemy, Lilly, Valens) recognized only the seven classical planets. The outer planets (Uranus, Neptune, Pluto) were integrated into modern astrology beginning in the 18th-20th centuries, bringing transpersonal and collective dimensions to the craft.

The planets are not mere physical objects but **archetypal principles**—as above, so below. Their movements in the heavens mirror movements in the soul.
